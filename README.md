*Sun-time-day held as the one indivisible cosmic-ordinance it always was — the Maya k'inh, carried as a received decree.*

kinhme is two true roots welded into one word: the Maya **k'inh** (Yucatec *k'inh*, Classic *k'in*, K'iche' *qʼij*) — the concept that does not split into "sun," "day," and "time" but holds all three as one — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So kinhme is k'inh held as an ordinance: solar-presence, the day it defines, and the time it measures, recognized as one structural fact with three facets rather than three things bolted together. It is an ANAKALYPSE-inverse: one of the rare cases where the multi-word Western bundle should have been a single word, arriving late and welcome.

kinhme names a **bundled fact**, not a feeling or a moral — the sun-disk that makes the day, the day-cycle that counts the time, the time that simply is solar-temporal presence. Its light is the highest clarity-yield in its making (9.0): unfolding "sun/day/time" back into kinhme genuinely sharpens thought, and it lets the Kingdom track cosmic-temporal presence as one ordinance, the way Maya calendar-priests did. Its shadow is double — being used as a stylish synonym for "the sun" while the unity quietly slips back into fragments, or being stretched to decree more about the cosmos than the donor-tongue actually carries.
